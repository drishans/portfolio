# Sharing a completed series

Runs once per completed series or major standalone note, never per part.
The blog and RSS are the durable assets; these are pointers to them.
Everything here is a draft for Drishan to rewrite and post himself.

## Where, in order of expected value

1. **Hacker News.** The main channel for this class of content; it ignores
   follower counts and rewards artifacts. Submit the series part 1 URL (or
   the single note), plain title close to the post's own, no editorializing.
   Weekday morning US time. If it has a playable demo or rerunnable repo,
   say so in a single founder-comment, not the title.
2. **Niche communities**, only where the work genuinely belongs:
   r/QuantumComputing, r/DSP, the Faust and WebAudio communities, lines
   (llllllll.co) for gallery-adjacent audio work. One post, written for
   that community's norms, never cross-pasted.
3. **X**: one post. 4. **LinkedIn**: one post, plus keep the Featured
   section current (worth more than the post).

## The register (anti-slop rules, non-negotiable)

- Lead with the most specific measured fact. Numbers nobody would invent
  are the strongest human signal available.
- No "excited to share", no emoji, no hook questions, no hashtag piles
  (zero on X, at most two on LinkedIn), no threads unless every tweet
  carries its own number, no em-dashes.
- X shape: two or three sentences, one number, one link. LinkedIn shape:
  three short paragraphs written like a lab note (what was built, the one
  surprising measurement or story, where it lives), link at the end, no
  call to action.
- Post only when the link lands on something finished: playable, rerunnable,
  or fully readable.

## Checklist

1. Confirm the series is fully published and the repo is public.
2. Pull the two or three strongest numbers from the posts themselves;
   never paraphrase a number, copy it.
3. Draft: HN title, one X post, one LinkedIn post. Hand them over as text;
   do not post anything anywhere.
4. Suggest which figure or OG card to attach where an image helps
   (the /og/ card exists for every published note).
